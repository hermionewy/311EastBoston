Understand Easties from Bos: 311

BY YAN WU

Boston residents could call 311, Mayor's office, anytime request for non-emergency city services and information.

There are 12964 requests for non-emergency city service through Bos:311 in East Boston in 2017. The issues mentioned the most include street cleaning, highway maintenance, sanitation, traffic, building, trees, street lights, recycling, housing and animals.

This map randomly displays the 311 requests related to East Boston in 2017.

2017
12:00 AM

Traffic

Traffic related issues have always been complained the most.

Enforcement & abandoned vehicles got the most frequently complains from the East Boston residents. In 2017, 2313 requests are related to it.

The Boston officials said "Abandoned vehicles issues are are safety hazards and they blight neighborhoods."

Among all neighborhoods, Eagle Hill has seen the most complaints.

Another traffic related problem is about highways. Three highways past East Boston: Massachusetts Route 1A, Interstate 90 (I-90)/Mass Turnpike, and Massachusetts Route 145.

There are 1388 requests for highway maintenance, among which, many happened along three routes - Meridian Street, Bennington Street, and Chelsea Street.

Environment

Judging from the 311 requests, street cleaning is the second most complained issue by the East Boston residents. In 2017, 1671 requests are related to it.

Residents at Eagle Hill and Jefferies Point have sent more requests than Orient Heights. Especially, Maverick Street residents have seen an unusual amount of requests about street cleaning.

Recycling and Sanitation service requests were also popular. Orient Heights residents showed more passion for these services than street cleaning.

Public Equipment

Street lights, Signs & Signals, Park Maintenance & Safety were also frequently complained issues, which added up to nearly 1700 requests.

There were more than 1000 complaints about housing and building issues, mostly happened in Eagle Hill and Jefferies Point.

SOURCE: Boston 311 Service Requests
